# Core Loop

One page. No UI. One question: why does someone open Lighthouse tomorrow?

---

## The Honest Answer

Not because they're afraid of losing something (no streak).
Not because we told them to (no push nagging).
Not because there's new content waiting (there's always new content — every app has that).

They open it because **yesterday made them feel a little more like themselves**,
and that's a feeling worth having again.

That's the whole loop. Everything else is implementation detail.

---

## The Mechanism, in Human Terms

1. **Receive** — an affirmation arrives, same as any app in this category.
   This is what they came for. It should never feel like less than what
   I Am already gives them.

2. **Read** — a few seconds. Low effort, matches the category's proven
   simplicity. This step alone is enough, most days, for most people.

3. **Reflect (optional)** — a quiet door, not a requirement: *has something
   in your own life reminded you this might already be true?* Most days,
   most people won't walk through it. That has to be completely fine.

4. **Remember** — sometimes, not every day, the app hands something back:
   a real thing they wrote once, resurfaced at a moment they weren't
   expecting it. This is the step nothing else in the category does. It's
   also the step most likely to be the actual reason someone tells a
   friend about Lighthouse.

5. **Return tomorrow** — not because the app asked. Because somewhere in
   steps 1–4, something landed that felt true rather than generic.

---

## Why This Is Different From "Engagement"

Most retention loops are built on a fear: *don't lose your streak, don't
miss today's content, don't fall behind.* That fear works, which is why so
many apps use it — and it's exactly the mechanism our Charter refuses.

Lighthouse's loop has no fear in it. The only thing pulling someone back is
the memory that it felt good last time — specifically, that it felt *true*
last time, not just pleasant. That's a slower loop to build than a streak
counter. It's also the only version of retention this company is allowed
to build, per the Charter, and it happens to be the one least likely to be
copied by anyone reshuffling a library of stock affirmations.

---

## The One Sentence Every Future Feature Gets Tested Against

**Does this make tomorrow's "Remember" moment more likely to feel true, or
does it just make today's "Receive" moment louder?**

If a feature only makes the loudest step louder, it's optimizing for
attention, not identity — and it doesn't belong in Lighthouse, regardless
of how good it looks on a roadmap slide.
