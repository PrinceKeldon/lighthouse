# 01 — The Experience Manifesto

Status: v1.0 — frozen alongside the rest of Foundation
Depends on: 00-charter.md

---

## 1. Why Experiences Matter

People rarely remember software. They remember how software made them feel.

We are not designing screens. We are designing moments.

A notification. A quiet pause before bed. A sentence that arrives on the
hardest day. A welcome after weeks away.

If people remember Lighthouse years later, it will not be because of
beautiful typography or clever features. It will be because, at the right
moment, it helped them remember they mattered.

Every interaction is therefore an act of care.

---

## 2. The Emotional Journey

Instead of a user journey, we build an emotional journey. This table
alone influences hundreds of future decisions.

| Moment | Desired Feeling |
|---|---|
| First Open | Safe |
| First Affirmation | Seen |
| End of Onboarding | Hopeful |
| Returning Tomorrow | Welcome |
| Missing a Week | Accepted |
| Reading During a Difficult Day | Understood |
| Going Premium | Confident |
| Cancelling | Respected |

---

## 3. Moments That Matter

**Returning after 47 days.** Most apps say *"Welcome Back!"* We don't. We
say something like: *"It's good to see you again. We've been here whenever
you were ready."* That difference matters.

**Missing reminders.** We never say *"You missed yesterday."* Instead:
*"Today is a new beginning."*

**Subscription.** We never create urgency. No "Only today!" No countdowns.
No fake scarcity. Trust first, always.

---

## 4. Our Voice

Not a style guide. A personality.

We are: warm, gentle, grounded, hopeful, quiet, present.

Never preachy. Never exaggerated. Never performative. Never overly
cheerful when someone may be hurting.

---

## 5. Our Behaviour

How the product behaves, not just how it speaks:

We wait. We don't interrupt. We ask permission. We explain ourselves. We
admit mistakes. We apologize clearly. We never manipulate.

---

## 6. Things We Refuse To Do

Boundaries define character.

- We refuse to shame users into returning.
- We refuse to create anxiety through notifications.
- We refuse to optimize for addiction.
- We refuse to confuse pricing.
- We refuse to pretend AI understands emotions it cannot understand.
- We refuse to exploit vulnerability for engagement.

This section should make some investors uncomfortable. Good.

---

## 7. The Lighthouse Test

Every feature must answer five questions:

1. Does this help someone remember they matter?
2. Does it increase trust?
3. Would we feel comfortable explaining this decision face-to-face with
   someone using the app during one of the hardest weeks of their life?
4. Does it reduce anxiety?
5. Would we still build it if nobody outside the company ever knew we
   built it?

If not, it probably doesn't belong.
