## Why a Lighthouse

A lighthouse doesn't rescue ships. It doesn't tell them where to go. It
doesn't steer the wheel. It simply reminds them where safe harbor is.

That's close to what this company believes about the people who will use
this product:

**We don't create someone's worth. We help them remember it.**

Every document in this repository, every product decision, every line of
copy, exists downstream of that one sentence. If a future decision can't
trace back to it, it doesn't belong in Lighthouse.

---

## Mission

To help people build a trustworthy relationship with themselves — not by
telling them who to become, but by helping them notice the evidence,
already in their own lives, of who they already are.

---

## Who We Serve

People searching for confidence, hope, motivation, self-love, and
encouragement — the same 10M+ people who have already told the market,
through their downloads, that they want this. We are not inventing a new
need. We are trying to meet an existing one with more care than it has
been met with so far. (Full personas: `05-user-personas.md`.)

## Foundational Principles

1. **Evidence over exposure.** Being told something is not the same as
   having proof of it. Where the category defaults to the former,
   Lighthouse is built to quietly offer the latter.
2. **Trust is the product.** Every erosion of trust identified in
   `03-market-research.md` — paywall flips, notifications that outlive
   consent, manufactured urgency — is something Lighthouse refuses on
   principle, not just on strategy.
3. **Quiet, not loud.** No streaks, no gamification, no engagement
   mechanics borrowed from products designed to be hard to put down.
   Retention here is earned by something feeling true, not by fear of
   losing progress.
4. **Privacy by default.** What someone writes about themselves is theirs.
   Private unless they choose otherwise, never a surface for advertising,
   never mined to sell them something else.
5. **Respect the market, differentiate the experience.** We enter where
   the demand already is (Layer 1) and win through how it feels to use the
   product (Layer 2) — not by trying to convince people they wanted
   something different than what they searched for. (Full reasoning:
   `02-product-strategy.md`.)
6. **Documentation is part of the product.** If it's important enough to
   discuss, it's important enough to document. Every significant decision
   gets written down — in a numbered doc or an ADR — so six months from now
   no one has to debate from memory why something is the way it is.

## The Lighthouse Test

Every feature, before it's built, answers five questions (developed in
full in `01-experience-manifesto.md`, restated here because it's the
filter this whole company runs through):

1. Does this help someone remember they matter?
2. Does it increase trust?
3. Would we feel comfortable explaining this decision face-to-face with
   someone using the app during one of the hardest weeks of their life?
4. Does it reduce anxiety?
5. Would we still build it if nobody outside the company ever knew we
   built it?

If the answer to any of these is no, it probably doesn't belong.

## What We Refuse To Do

Full list and reasoning live in `01-experience-manifesto.md` §6. At the
Charter level, the commitment is simple: we refuse to shame, pressure,
manipulate, or exploit vulnerability for engagement or revenue — even when
doing so would be more profitable, and even when it's an industry norm.
This section should make some investors uncomfortable. Good.

## How We Work

- **Every document is immutable history.** Once a document reaches v1.0, we
  don't rewrite it — we version it (v1.0 → v1.1 → v2.0), so the repository
  can always show how Lighthouse's thinking evolved rather than presenting
  a false record of having always known the answer.
- **Every commit answers one question:** does this move the Charter closer
  to reality? Commit messages explain the human reason for a change, not
  just the mechanical one.
- **Every meeting — design, engineering, pricing, growth — starts with the
  same question:** *Who are we helping today?* Not what's on the sprint.
  Not what's the KPI. We begin with a person, because Lighthouse isn't
  being built for "users." It's being built for people, each carrying a
  story we may never know.

## Our Internal Motto

**Build with care. Ship with courage.**

Care reminds us of this Charter. Courage reminds us not to spend six
months polishing before helping the first person.

---

## Definition of Success

Not downloads, not engagement time, not streak length. Milestone 0 is
named "Remember," not "MVP," on purpose — because the only question that
matters at the start is the one this whole Charter exists to answer:

**Can we help someone remember that they matter?**

If yes, everything else — the business, the roadmap, the growth — is worth
building on top of. If no, nothing else matters yet.
